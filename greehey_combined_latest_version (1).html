Dependency Risk Insights
Pay It Forward — Operational Dependency
Received 3 grants totaling $125K, all tied to Haven for Hope residential programming. This suggests the Foundation is essentially underwriting their operations. A sudden grant exit could defund the program entirely. Request full budget disclosure.
Faith Kitchen — Dual-Cycle Recipient
$100K across two cycles (June + November). Faith Kitchen is a small food ministry — this level of support likely represents 30–60% of their annual budget. Strong mission fit, but dependency is real. Consider a capacity-building grant to diversify their fundraising.
IRS Public Support Test — "Tipping" Watch
Several smaller grantees flagged above receive grants estimated at 30–70% of their operating budget from this Foundation. Under IRC §509(a)(1)/(2), an organization that receives more than 33% of support from a single donor over a 5-year rolling period risks losing public charity status ("tipping"). Faith Kitchen, Pay It Forward, AGORA Ministries, Corazon Ministries, and West Avenue Compassion warrant a Form 990 schedule B review before renewal to ensure continued public support test compliance.
Recommendations
🔴
Adopt a dependency policy. Best practice: no single grantee should receive more than 20–30% of their total operating budget from one foundation. For any org where this is suspected, request a copy of their most recent Form 990 or audited financials before renewing.
🟠
Stage exit strategies for high-dependency orgs. If the Foundation ever needs to reduce or exit a grant, high-dependency orgs need 12–18 months notice and transition support — not just a grant decline letter. Consider adding this language to grant agreements.
🔵
Consider capacity-building supplements. For orgs rated High or Medium risk, a supplemental $5–10K grant earmarked for "fundraising infrastructure" is a high-leverage intervention that reduces dependency over time.
